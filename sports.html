Volleyball

Volleyball is a sport where two teams of six players use their hands to hit a ball back and forth over a net.

The goal is to hit the ball into the other team's court without letting it touch the ground. Here are some facts about volleyball.

The game starts with a coin toss to determine which team serves first. The server hits the ball over the net from behind the baseline. The receiving team has three hits to return the ball over the net. The rally continues until a team wins by grounding the ball in the opponent's court or the other team commits a fault. The winning team scores a point and serves the ball next.

Soccer

Soccer is a team sport where two teams of 11 players compete to score goals by moving the ball into the opponent's goal with their feet, head, and chest.

The game is divided into two 45-minute halves and is controlled by a referee and two linesmen. A goal is scored when the ball crosses the goal line.

The origins of soccer can be traced back over 2,000 years to ancient China, Greece, and Rome. However, the modern version of the game originated in 19th century England. The first modern soccer game was played in London in 1863.

Tennis

Tennis is a racket sport where players hit a ball over a net and into their opponent's side of the court.

The goal is to hit the ball in a way that the opponent can't return it, scoring points.

Tennis originated in Britain in the 1870s from earlier games that used rackets and balls. The first world lawn tennis championship was held in 1877 at Wimbledon.
